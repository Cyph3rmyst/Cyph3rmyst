Why cybersecurity is a good funding career

Cybersecurity is one of the fields where experience and specialized skills can significantly increase earning potential over time. Demand remains strong globally, including for SOC analysts, incident responders, malware analysts, and digital forensics professionals.

That doesn't guarantee a particular salary, but it does mean there is a realistic path to increasing your income as your skills deepen.

Keep aviation alive while studying cyber

You don't have to wait until flight school to think like a pilot.

Each month you can:

Read aviation material.

Watch accident investigation videos to understand decision-making.

Practice aviation phraseology.

Follow KCAA updates.

Check airline cadet openings.

Visit Wilson Airport occasionally if practical.


Those habits cost little but keep the goal active.

One final perspective

You're 20 years old.

Commercial pilots start at many different ages. Some begin in their early 20s, others in their late 20s or even 30s after careers in engineering, the military, or other professions. There isn't a single "correct" timeline.

✈️ Pilot is the destination.

If you steadily grow your cyber career while consistently setting aside money for flight training and keeping aviation knowledge fresh, you'll be advancing toward the same destination from two complementary directions.
