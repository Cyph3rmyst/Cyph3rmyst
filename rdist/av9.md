For a Class 1 Aviation Medical (required for a Commercial Pilot Licence), the Aviation Medical Examiner (AME) evaluates whether you're medically fit to fly safely. KCAA follows ICAO aviation medicine standards. 

Here's what is commonly assessed:

👁️ Vision

Distance vision (visual acuity)

Near vision

Colour vision (usually with an Ishihara colour blindness test)

Depth perception (where required)

Eye health examination

Glasses/contact lenses assessment if you use them. 


👂 Hearing

Hearing assessment

Pure-tone audiometry (hearing test in a soundproof booth). 


❤️ Cardiovascular (Heart)

Blood pressure

Pulse

Heart examination

Electrocardiogram (ECG) to assess heart rhythm and electrical activity. 


🫁 Respiratory (Lungs)

Lung examination

Spirometry (lung function test), especially if there is a history of asthma or other respiratory conditions. 


🧠 Neurological

Nervous system examination

Balance and coordination

Reflexes

Screening for neurological disorders. 


🩺 General Physical Examination

The AME checks your overall health, including:

Height and weight

General physical fitness

Abdomen

Limbs and joints

Spine

Skin

General appearance. 


🩸 Blood Tests

These may include:

Haemoglobin (to check for anaemia)

Cholesterol/lipid profile

Other tests if indicated by your medical history. 


🚻 Urine Test

Checks for:

Sugar (diabetes)

Protein

Blood

Other abnormalities. 


📋 Medical History Review

You'll be asked about:

Previous illnesses

Surgeries

Current medications

Hospital admissions

Family medical history

Mental health history. 


🧠 Mental Health

The examiner assesses whether there are any medical or psychological conditions that could affect safe flying. If necessary, additional evaluation may be requested. 


---

For someone aiming for an airline career, these are the major areas to keep healthy over the coming years:

✅ Eyesight

✅ Colour vision

✅ Hearing

✅ Heart health

✅ Blood pressure

✅ Lung function

✅ General physical fitness

✅ Healthy body weight

✅ Mental well-being

✅ No uncontrolled medical conditions


Since you're only 20 years old and planning your journey over several years, maintaining healthy habits—like your endurance running, getting enough sleep, eating well, and avoiding smoking or recreational drugs—can support your overall health as you work toward meeting the standards for a Class 1 medical. Your medical certificate, however, will always depend on the assessment of an approved Aviation Medical Examiner under KCAA regulations.
