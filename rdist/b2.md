The political situation in Kuamar is the central reason Raven is deployed. Although Kuamar is fictional, its political system is designed to resemble a modern authoritarian petrostate in the Middle East.

1. An Entrenched Dictatorship

Kuamar is ruled by President Bibi Rashida and her husband Omar Al-Bakr, who have maintained power for roughly two decades through military force, surveillance, and repression. The government is effectively a dictatorship rather than a democracy. 

2. Power is Concentrated at the Top

The regime controls nearly every major institution:

The armed forces.

Intelligence and internal security.

Oil production and export.

Government ministries.

State media.


Rather than independent institutions, these organizations exist primarily to preserve the ruling family's power.

3. Political Repression

The government suppresses opposition through:

Arrests of dissidents.

Secret police and intelligence surveillance.

Military enforcement.

Elimination of political rivals.


Throughout the missions, many of Raven's targets are senior officials who enable this system rather than frontline soldiers.

4. Regional Destabilization

The dictatorship is no longer viewed as merely a domestic problem. Intelligence indicates that Kuamar is preparing aggressive actions against neighboring states, creating the risk of a wider regional conflict. Since Kuamar is an oil-producing nation, such a war could severely disrupt global energy supplies and the international economy. This geopolitical threat is what prompts outside powers to authorize Raven's covert operation. 

5. Why Raven Is Sent Instead of an Army

An open invasion would likely:

Trigger a larger regional war.

Cause major civilian casualties.

Create diplomatic complications.


Instead, Raven is hired to conduct a deniable decapitation campaign—eliminating the leadership, military commanders, scientists, and infrastructure supporting the regime, with the goal of collapsing its ability to wage war. 

One interesting aspect of the game's writing is that Kuamar itself is not portrayed as the enemy. The distinction is between the country and the regime. The missions are aimed at dismantling the dictatorship while implying that ordinary civilians are victims of the government's rule rather than the intended targets of Raven's operation.
