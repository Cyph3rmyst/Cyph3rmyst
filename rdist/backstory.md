CHAPTER I — THE LAST BULLET

The world never saw Raven arrive in Kuamar.

That was the point.

No aircraft crossed the skies with flags painted on its wings. No army entered through the borders. No declaration of war was made. The operation existed in the shadows, where governments denied involvement and enemies disappeared without understanding why.

Raven was the ghost sent into the heart of a nation controlled by fear.

Kuamar had been under the iron grip of President Bibi Rashida and her inner circle for decades. The regime controlled everything: the military, intelligence networks, industrial facilities, energy systems, and the flow of information. The people lived under surveillance while the ruling elite transformed Kuamar into a fortress.

Raven's contracts slowly dismantled the machine.

The generals fell.

The intelligence commanders vanished.

The weapons programs were destroyed.

The regime's communication networks went dark.

Every shot was calculated. Every movement had a purpose.

The world watched from a distance as Kuamar's strongest figures were removed one by one.

But governments misunderstood one thing.

They believed removing the people controlling the system would destroy the system itself.

They were wrong.

The final mission ended at Rashida Qalat.

The old regime had built a second empire.

From information.
