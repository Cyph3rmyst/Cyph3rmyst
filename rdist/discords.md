Since you're starting from zero and your goal is to become a self-sponsored airline pilot, I'd prioritize communities where you can learn from student pilots, instructors, and professional pilots—not just aviation fans.

Here are the ones I'd join first.

🥇 1. The ATPL Network

Best overall recommendation

Around 15,000+ members

Student pilots, CPL students, ATPL students, and licensed pilots

Ground school help

Medical discussions

Flight school advice

Airline career discussions


This is probably the closest to the path you're aiming for. 


---

🥈 2. Aviation Haven

10,000+ members

Active aviation news

Certified pilots and instructors

Career discussions

Aviation networking

Good mix of beginners and experienced aviators





---

🥉 3. Young Aviators

This one fits your age almost perfectly.

It welcomes aspiring pilots and provides:

Ground school questions

Career advice

Flight simulator discussions

Networking with other young people pursuing aviation





---

4. Student Pilots Club

Smaller community, but that's often an advantage because conversations are more personal.

Focus:

Future pilots

Study together

Ask beginner questions

Practice aviation English and communications





---

5. The Flight Lounge

Good if you also enjoy flight simulators.

You'll find:

Student pilots

Real-world pilots

ATC communications

Group events

Aviation networking





---

6. PilotDebrief Discord

If you enjoy learning from accident investigations and understanding decision-making, this is a great complement.

They discuss:

NTSB reports

Aviation safety

Pilot decision-making

Questions from beginners





---

and people becoming better pilots.


Those are the two worlds you're intentionally building toward.

One small suggestion for using these communities well: spend more time reading than posting at first. Watch how experienced pilots discuss training, medicals, and careers. Over time, you'll develop a feel for the profession long before you begin flight school.
