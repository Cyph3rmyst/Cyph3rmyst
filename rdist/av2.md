While in campus, what can you do?

1. Study the aviation basics

You don't need to be in flight school yet to start learning subjects like:

Principles of Flight

Aircraft Systems

Meteorology

Air Navigation

Aviation English

Human Performance

Air Law


When you eventually begin flight school, these subjects will already feel familiar. KCAA describes the PPL as the foundation of all later pilot training. 


---

2. Follow KCAA-approved flight schools

Keep a list of approved training organizations and check their announcements occasionally. New intakes, partnerships, or scholarships can appear. 


---

3. Apply for cadet programmes whenever you're eligible

Even if you don't expect to get in, applying gives you experience with:

aptitude tests

interviews

psychometric assessments

medical requirements


Many cadet programmes worldwide are highly competitive, but applying costs far less than wondering "what if?" later. 


---

4. Keep your Class 1 medical in mind

Don't necessarily rush to get one now, but stay healthy enough that when you're ready to start training, there are no surprises. Aviation medical fitness remains a key requirement for a Commercial Pilot Licence. 


---

5. Improve your aviation English

English is the language of international aviation. Strong communication will help in training, licensing, and airline interviews. 


---
